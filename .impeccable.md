## Design Context

### Users
SMBs (small-to-medium businesses) in Greece. Decision-makers — owners, operations managers, CTOs at growing companies — looking for a trusted technology partner to implement Salesforce or build custom software. They may not be deeply technical but they are results-oriented and value clear communication.

### Brand Personality
Adaptable, fresh, inspiring. The site should feel like talking to someone who genuinely gets business challenges and has the technical chops to solve them — not like a faceless agency or a generic "tech" brand.

### Aesthetic Direction
Impressive but accessible. Greek SMB owners should feel confident this person can handle serious work, while also feeling that reaching out isn't intimidating. Not corporate cold, not startup chaotic. Somewhere between a premium boutique consultancy and an approachable expert. Light or dark theme should serve the "fresh, inspiring" brief — not default dark-for-cool's-sake.

### Design Principles
1. **Confidence without arrogance** — Typography and layout should convey authority; copy and whitespace should keep it human
2. **Fresh over familiar** — Avoid the default AI aesthetic (cyan-on-dark, Inter+SpaceGrotesk, glassmorphism cards, gradient text)
3. **Accessible impression** — High visual quality that doesn't feel like it requires a large budget to engage with
4. **Local context** — Greek SMBs respond to directness and results; avoid vague buzzwords
5. **Motion with purpose** — Entrance animations yes, gratuitous floating decorations no
